I. States of Matter

  • Definition: Matter with a definite shape and volume.
  • Properties:
    • Particles are closely packed in a fixed arrangement.
    • Vibrate but do not move from place to place.
    • Incompressible and rigid.
  • Definition: Matter that has a definite volume but takes the shape of its container.
  • Properties:
    • Particles are close together but can move/slide past one another.
    • Fluid and can flow.
    • Slightly compressible.
  • Definition: Matter without a definite shape or volume.
  • Properties:
    • Particles are far apart and move freely.
    • Fill any container they're in.
    • Highly compressible.
  • Definition: Ionized gas with free electrons.
  • Properties:
    • Conducts electricity.
    • Affected by magnetic fields.
    • Found in stars and fluorescent lights.
